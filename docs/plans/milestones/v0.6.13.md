# v0.6.13 — Living Place Simulation v1 (occupancy + latent hooks)

**Target**: TBD.
**Bar**: "When I walk into a bar, restaurant, road, office, or public place, it feels populated by people who plausibly belong there — *and woven through that crowd are threads I can pull*. The world both reflects the arcs I'm already carrying and quietly offers new ones, without turning every background person into a full agent."
**Budget**: ~10-14 hours focused work.

Patch on top of `v0.6.12`. v0.6.9-v0.6.12 strengthened the narrator/NPC contract, intent audit, scene pacing, audio cache, and audio latency. v0.6.13 moves the world model one layer outward in two coupled steps:

1. **Occupancy substrate** — places generate a bounded, plausible population (staff, patrons, traffic, passersby) from structured state instead of relying on the narrator to improvise crowds.
2. **Latent-hook bridge** — that population, plus the place and the player's **story dossier**, deterministically surfaces *encounter hooks* that connect the ambient world to the dossier. A populated bar is not just texture; it can contain a lead on a quest you already hold, or the opening of a new one.

This is the first concrete slice toward the larger Westworld-style goal: a world that feels alive **and** pulls the player toward stories. It does **not** add a full background world simulator, relationship graph, semantic memory, cron-driven NPC maintenance, or an active pacing director. The director (escalation) half is deferred to `v0.6.14` (see *Explicit cuts*).

## Why

1. **Places are still too empty by default.** Current narrator state includes the player, named NPCs in the current place, tracked off-scene NPCs, and dossier pressure, but it has no structured concept of ordinary patrons, staff, traffic, commuters, regulars, or passersby.
2. **The app needs crowds without durable-row explosion.** A restaurant may need ten visible people, but only one or two should become persistent characters. Creating full `characters` rows for every bartender, diner, and driver will bloat state and prompts.
3. **The world is alive but inert.** Even once a place is populated, nothing connects that population to the player's story. The dossier system (threads/clues/objectives, with stakes/rewards/hidden pressure) already exerts narrative pull through the narrator's STATE block — but there is **no bridge** between the ambient world and the dossier. A bartender in a snapshot is texture; in the world we want, he can be a quest-giver.
4. **NPC agency needs a staging layer below full promotion.** The existing `npc` / `local` / `nearby` / `distant` / `dormant` tiers work for known characters; we need an earlier lightweight layer for anonymous or semi-named people who can be promoted *only if play makes them matter*.
5. **Westworld-style liveliness needs deterministic substrate.** "The world has pulse, and the pulse leads somewhere" cannot live only in prompts. The runtime needs authoritative state for who is plausibly present, what they are doing, and which encounter hooks are available — computed in code, not improvised by an LLM.

## Design stance: Decide → Render → Record (no new agent)

"Pull the player toward stories" hides three jobs. v0.6.13 assigns each to an existing owner, and **adds no new agent**:

| Verb | Owner in v0.6.13 | Responsibility |
|---|---|---|
| **Decide** | Deterministic code (`place-population.ts`) | *Which* occupants are present and *which* hooks surface, computed from place profile + templates + world time + the active dossier. No LLM in the turn path. |
| **Render** | **Narrator** (existing) | Receives finished, compact occupancy + 1-3 hook cues in STATE and weaves them into prose. It does **not** score relevance, pick occupants, or escalate. Same role it already plays for occupancy/dossier today. |
| **Record** | **Archivist** (existing, async) | When the player engages a promotable occupant carrying a hook, promotes it to a durable character and *binds* the hook to the dossier (advance an existing thread, or create a new one). |

The narrator is **not** overloaded: deciding stays in code, recording stays in the archivist. The **escalation** job — noticing the player is drifting and actively pushing a hook, raising stakes, sending an NPC to find them — is a *pacing decision* the narrator must never make. That is the **Story Conductor's** job (today a stub) and is deferred to `v0.6.14`.

## Scope

Add a lightweight place-population layer that builds a bounded occupancy snapshot for the current place **before** narrator generation, and deterministically derives encounter hooks that connect that snapshot to the story dossier. The snapshot is authoritative for the current scene/turn but mostly ephemeral: staff, patrons, traffic, vehicles, crowds, and ambience do **not** become durable characters unless the player interacts with them and the archivist promotes them.

```
schema (migration v22):
  place_profiles              NEW. Per-place capacity, hours, roles, traffic, tags, encounter rules.
  population_templates        NEW. Reusable world/place role templates for lightweight NPCs/groups,
                              optionally carrying a seed-hook premise.
  place_occupancy_snapshots   NEW. Per-scene/turn visible population + structured encounter hooks.
  story_threads               + relevance_tags_json. Lets the matcher connect a thread to a place/occupant.

runtime:
  buildPlaceOccupancySnapshot(worldId, playerTurnId)   deterministic occupancy + hook derivation
  formatStateBlock(...)                                 includes occupancy + hooks in a compact section
  archivist                                             promotes interacted occupants; binds hooks to dossier
```

### Hooks, concretely

Two hook kinds, both derived deterministically (tag overlap), both bound by the archivist only on engagement:

- **Continuation hook** — surfaces an occupant relevant to one of the player's **active threads**. Relevance is decided by intersecting that thread's `relevance_tags_json` with the place profile's tags ∪ the present promotable occupant's template tags. References a concrete `thread_id`. *("The bartender keeps glancing at the name in your file.")*
- **Seed hook** — surfaces a promotable occupant drawn from a `population_template` that carries a `seed_premise`. Offers a **new** thread if engaged. Selection is weighted and seeded for stability; the world always has *something* on offer without spamming. *("A man at the end of the bar has been waiting for someone like you.")*

## Migration sketch (v22)

```sql

  id                    INTEGER PRIMARY KEY AUTOINCREMENT,
  world_id              INTEGER NOT NULL REFERENCES worlds(id) ON DELETE CASCADE,
  place_id              INTEGER NOT NULL REFERENCES places(id) ON DELETE CASCADE,
  profile_kind          TEXT NOT NULL,
  capacity_min          INTEGER NOT NULL DEFAULT 0,
  capacity_max          INTEGER NOT NULL DEFAULT 6,
  typical_roles_json    TEXT NOT NULL DEFAULT '[]',
  open_hours_json       TEXT,
  traffic_level         TEXT NOT NULL DEFAULT 'medium'
                        CHECK (traffic_level IN ('none','low','medium','high','surge')),
  ambience_tags_json    TEXT NOT NULL DEFAULT '[]',
  match_tags_json       TEXT NOT NULL DEFAULT '[]',   -- tags used by the hook matcher
  encounter_rules_json  TEXT NOT NULL DEFAULT '[]',
  created_at            TEXT NOT NULL DEFAULT (datetime('now')),
  updated_at            TEXT NOT NULL DEFAULT (datetime('now')),
  UNIQUE(world_id, place_id)
);

CREATE TABLE population_templates (
  id                    INTEGER PRIMARY KEY AUTOINCREMENT,
  world_id              INTEGER NOT NULL REFERENCES worlds(id) ON DELETE CASCADE,
  place_profile_kind    TEXT,
  role                  TEXT NOT NULL,
  label                 TEXT NOT NULL,
  description           TEXT,
  behavior_tags_json    TEXT NOT NULL DEFAULT '[]',
  match_tags_json       TEXT NOT NULL DEFAULT '[]',   -- tags used by the hook matcher
  seed_premise          TEXT,                          -- non-null => can carry a SEED hook
  promotable            INTEGER NOT NULL DEFAULT 0,
  weight                INTEGER NOT NULL DEFAULT 1,
  created_at            TEXT NOT NULL DEFAULT (datetime('now')),
  updated_at            TEXT NOT NULL DEFAULT (datetime('now'))
);

CREATE TABLE place_occupancy_snapshots (
  id                    INTEGER PRIMARY KEY AUTOINCREMENT,
  world_id              INTEGER NOT NULL REFERENCES worlds(id) ON DELETE CASCADE,
  place_id              INTEGER NOT NULL REFERENCES places(id) ON DELETE CASCADE,
  scene_id              INTEGER REFERENCES scenes(id) ON DELETE SET NULL,
  source_turn_id        INTEGER REFERENCES turns(id) ON DELETE SET NULL,
  world_time            TEXT,
  occupancy_json        TEXT NOT NULL,
  created_at            TEXT NOT NULL DEFAULT (datetime('now'))
);

-- Bridge to the dossier: a thread declares what places/topics it is "about".
ALTER TABLE story_threads ADD COLUMN relevance_tags_json TEXT NOT NULL DEFAULT '[]';

CREATE INDEX place_profiles_world_place ON place_profiles(world_id, place_id);
CREATE INDEX population_templates_world_role ON population_templates(world_id, role);
CREATE INDEX occupancy_world_place_scene ON place_occupancy_snapshots(world_id, place_id, scene_id, id);
```

## The deterministic builder (`buildPlaceOccupancySnapshot`)

Pure, testable, no LLM. Steps:

1. **Resolve / infer the place profile.** If a `place_profiles` row exists for the place, use it. Otherwise infer one lazily from `places.kind`, name keywords, and setting (e.g. `kind='bar'` → capacity 4-12, roles `[staff, patrons, regulars]`, traffic `medium`, tags `[bar, nightlife, drink, social]`). Inferred profiles may be persisted so the room is stable on return.
2. **Compute density** from `traffic_level` × world-time band (`open_hours_json`). Bands only affect density and role mix in v1 — no per-NPC schedules.
3. **Select occupancy groups** from `population_templates` matching `place_profile_kind`, weighted by `weight`, bounded by `[capacity_min, capacity_max]`. Mark a minority `promotable`.
4. **Derive continuation hooks** (the bridge): for each **active** thread in the dossier, intersect `thread.relevance_tags_json` with `profile.match_tags_json ∪ (template.match_tags_json for present promotable occupants)`. Non-empty intersection ⇒ candidate. Score by overlap size × thread importance/recency. Attach the top 1-2 to a specific promotable occupant (or to the place if none fits). `strength: 'strong'` when overlap is high, else `'ambient'`.
5. **Derive seed hooks**: if continuation hooks are sparse (< 1), draw 0-1 promotable occupant whose template has a non-null `seed_premise`, weighted by `weight`. Caps total hooks at **3**.
6. **Stamp a stable seed.** All weighted/random choices use a PRNG seeded from `hash(world_id, place_id, scene_id, world_time_band)`. Re-running for the same scene/time band reproduces the same room and hooks → satisfies "don't re-roll the room."

**Reuse rule:** while the player stays in the same scene, reuse the latest snapshot. Rebuild only when scene, place, world-time band, or a conflict/narration event changes occupancy.

## Runtime flow

```text
player submits turn
  | (player action persisted BEFORE streaming — unchanged)
  v
active place + scene resolved
  v
place profile + templates + world time + ACTIVE dossier threads (with relevance_tags)
  v
buildPlaceOccupancySnapshot  ->  deterministic groups + structured encounter_hooks
  | (persist snapshot row; reuse if same scene/time band)
  v
NPC agent plans named agent-tier NPCs (sees the same place reality)
  v
narrator STATE receives, in order:
  - named present characters
  - lightweight occupancy groups (density, 3-6 groups, traffic)
  - 1-3 encounter-hook cues (continuation + seed), as soft affordances
  - story dossier pressure
  v
narrator streams prose (RENDER only — no deciding)
  v
archivist (async) RECORDS:
  - tags any new/updated threads with relevance_tags
  - if the player engaged a promotable occupant: promote it -> durable character
    - continuation hook -> advance thread_id (add clue/objective, status nudge)
    - seed hook         -> create a NEW thread from premise (kind inferred), tagged
```

## Archivist binding (Record)

The archivist already parses narration into wiki/timeline/threads. v0.6.13 additions:

- **Tagging.** When the archivist creates or updates a thread, it emits `relevance_tags` (topic + place-kind tags). This is what later turns' matcher reads. Small prompt change; no new pipeline.
- **Occupant promotion.** The archivist receives the current snapshot's promotable occupants + hooks in its context. If the narration shows the player directly engaging one (talks to, names, follows, harms, helps, or the narrator made it story-relevant), it promotes that occupant to a durable `characters` row (current place + description derived from the occupant label/behavior).
- **Hook binding.** On promotion:
  - *Continuation* → add a clue or objective to `thread_id`, and/or nudge thread status, so the arc visibly advances.
  - *Seed* → create a new `story_threads` row from `premise` (kind inferred: quest/mystery/threat/relationship), `status='active'`, with `relevance_tags` seeded from the place/template tags.
- **Restraint.** Background occupants the player ignores are **never** promoted and leave no durable rows.

## Narrator prompt stance (Render)

- Occupancy and hooks are **visible scene reality**, not exposition. Use crowds for texture, witnesses, obstacles, and service interactions.
- Do **not** list every group unless the player scans the room.
- Hook `narrator_cue`s are **soft affordances**, not quest markers. Weave or ignore per scene logic; never surface mechanics vocabulary (no "quest", "objective", "hook", "thread").
- Continuation cues should feel like *the world remembering the player's arc*; seed cues like *an opening worth taking*. The player must be able to walk past either.

## Decisions

| Decision | Choice | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Milestone shape | Occupancy + latent-hook bridge | Directly serves the user's "alive **and** pulls toward stories" goal without pretending to solve full open-world simulation or the active director. |
| New agent? | None | Deciding is deterministic code; rendering is the existing narrator; recording is the existing archivist. Keeps the narrator a writer. |
| Pull model | Seed + escalate; **only seed ships here** | Escalation is an active pacing decision (drift detection, pushing the player). That is the Conductor's job → `v0.6.14`. |
| Crowd storage | Snapshot rows, not durable characters | Crowds need within-scene continuity and post-turn auditability, but must not permanently enlarge `characters` unless play promotes them. |
| Generation source | Deterministic code + templates | Cheaper, testable, and more consistent than calling an LLM on every place entry. |
| Hook location | Embedded in the occupancy snapshot (approach A) | Reuses the per-scene persistence already paid for; gives audit + stability for free; no new decoupled hook table (that is a director-era concern). |
| Hook targets | Continuation (existing threads) **and** seed (new threads) | World feels responsive to current arcs *and* offers fresh adventures — the Westworld feel. |
| Relevance match | Tag overlap | Deterministic, testable, serendipitous. Archivist already extracts threads; tagging them is a small prompt change. |
| Promotion rule | Archivist promotes only engaged occupants | A person becomes durable only when the player talks to, follows, names, harms, helps, or the narrator makes them story-relevant. |
| Snapshot timing | Build before NPC agent/narrator | NPC agent and narrator see the same place reality. Named present NPCs stay separate from lightweight occupancy. |
| Snapshot lifetime | Current scene/turn scoped, seeded PRNG | Reuse while in-scene; rebuild only on scene/place/time-band/conflict change. Seeded so repeats are stable. |
| Road/traffic handling | Same snapshot mechanism | Drivers, traffic, pedestrians, vehicles are occupancy groups; no separate v1 traffic simulator. |
| Place profiles | Backfill lazily | Infer from `places.kind`, name keywords, setting. Manual/editor tooling later. |

## Explicit cuts

- **Active director / escalation → `v0.6.14`.** No drift detection, no pushing hooks at the player, no off-screen story advancement, no scene-transition decisions. The Conductor stays a stub this milestone.
- **Full NPC schedules** — deferred. World time only affects density and role mix.
- **First-class relationship graph** — deferred.
- **Event clocks / story clocks** — deferred (a small `encounter_rules_json` field is the only nod).
- **Background cron simulation** — deferred. Runs in the chat path at scene/turn boundaries only.
- **Semantic memory / vector retrieval** — deferred.
- **Pathfinding / traffic simulation** — deferred. Roads get traffic occupancy, not routed vehicles.
- **Inspector UI for editing place profiles / templates / tags** — deferred. Seed defaults and tests first.
- **Decoupled hook table / hooks that follow the player** — deferred (approach B; a director-era capability).

## Accepted tradeoffs

- **Snapshots can be approximate.** A generated crowd may drift over long spans. Tolerable: snapshots are scene-bounded and refreshed at clear boundaries, with a seeded PRNG for stability.
- **Hook quality depends on tag quality.** Continuation hooks only fire when threads carry good `relevance_tags`. Early worlds with untagged threads will see fewer continuation hooks until the archivist backfills tags. Seed hooks (template-driven) cover the gap so the world is never inert.
- **Some liveliness is still narrator-mediated.** Code supplies population facts and hook cues; prose decides how much to foreground. Prompt discipline matters.
- **No true daily routine yet.** Known NPCs still use current place and journey state.
- **More state in the narrator prompt.** Occupancy + hooks must be aggressively compact: density, 3-6 groups, traffic, 1-3 hook cues. Never dump full JSON.
- **Promotion can miss subtle characters.** The archivist may fail to promote a background person the player cared about. The manual repair path remains the correction channel until heuristics mature.

## Exit criteria

1. `npm run lint` and `npm run type-check` pass.
2. `npm test` passes, including `place-population` coverage for: profile inference, density bounds, role mix, deterministic/seeded snapshot stability, road traffic groups, **tag-overlap continuation-hook selection**, **seed-hook selection when continuations are sparse**, and promotion-worthy occupant detection.
3. `package.json` and `package-lock.json` read `0.6.13` on the release branch. Restart `npm run dev` and confirm the header shows `v0.6.13`.
4. Fresh DB migration reaches v22 and `PRAGMA foreign_key_check` returns no rows.
5. Manual: enter a bar/restaurant and verify the narrator STATE includes a compact occupancy section (staff, patrons, density) and at least one hook cue.
6. Manual: enter or drive on a road and verify traffic/vehicle/pedestrian occupancy arrives without creating durable `characters` rows for every driver.
7. Manual (continuation): hold an active thread tagged for a place kind, enter such a place, and verify a **continuation** hook referencing that thread surfaces; engage the occupant and verify the archivist promotes it **and** advances the referenced thread (new clue/objective).
8. Manual (seed): enter a place whose templates carry a `seed_premise`, engage the promotable occupant, and verify the archivist creates a **new** tagged thread.
9. Manual (stability): stay in the same place for two turns and verify the snapshot and its hooks remain coherent rather than re-rolling.
10. Manual (variety): leave and enter a different kind of place and verify the role mix and hooks change appropriately.
11. Manual (restraint): walk past background occupants without engaging and verify no durable `characters` rows are created and no spurious threads appear.
12. Deploy to Railway and smoke one turn in production without 5xx, migration errors, or excessive prompt bloat.
13. Tag `v0.6.13` on `main`.

When all hold, v0.6.13 ships. The next living-world milestone (`v0.6.14`) wakes the **Story Conductor**: it adds the *escalate* half — drift detection, pushing latent hooks toward the player, and the first off-screen story advancement — taking the pacing decisions that must never live in the narrator.
