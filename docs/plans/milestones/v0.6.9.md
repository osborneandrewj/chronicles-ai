# v0.6.9 Milestone

**Target**: TBD.
**Bar**: "I can see what each agent-tier NPC intended to do, whether the narrator staged it, and what actually happened."
**Budget**: ~6-10 hours focused work.

v0.6.9 is the first concrete slice of the NPC/Narrator v2 direction from `combined-npc-narrator-design-evaluation-v2.md` and the practical Westworld-style architecture review. The current architecture is sound: narrator writes prose, NPC agent plans behavior, archivist records state. The weak spot is auditability. Today an NPC can plan something before narration, but there is no durable row that says whether the narrator staged, modified, ignored, or contradicted that plan. v0.6.9 adds that trail.

This is deliberately not the whole v2 proposal. The larger architecture is directionally right, but the current app already has a richer project-specific runtime than the generic sketch: tiered NPC attention, reveries, journey state, player canon, geography anchors, and an archivist correction channel. v0.6.9 should strengthen that system, not replace it. Relationship tables, memory visibility, narrator-blind secrets, and background reverie jobs are kept as follow-on work once intent/outcome tracking gives us real evidence about the NPC agent/narrator contract.

## Current Architecture Evaluation

What is working well:

1. **Separation of concerns is right.** Narrator owns prose, NPC Agent owns agent-tier behavior/planning, and Archivist owns state reconciliation.
2. **Append-only `turns` are the durable book.** This keeps replay, debugging, and canon recovery tractable.
3. **Tiered agency is pragmatic.** The current `npc` / `local` / `nearby` / `distant` / `dormant` vocabulary is more useful than a generic 0-3 scale because it encodes both attention and proximity.
4. **Archivist after narration is the correct authority model.** Prose wins; state follows.
5. **Player canon is cleanly separated.** The inspector correction channel keeps out-of-fiction repair text away from the main narrative.

Risks that matter next:

1. **State reconciliation risk.** The NPC Agent can update state before narration, but the Narrator can ignore or alter the plan. Without an intent ledger, the system cannot distinguish desire from canon.
2. **Memory bloat.** `memorable_facts`, `observations`, `recent_activity`, `private_beliefs`, and `reveries` are newline logs. Prompt formatting trims them, but storage and summarization are still unbounded.
3. **Secret management is not strong enough yet.** `private_beliefs` and `reveries` are private-ish, but they are not a tested visibility system where an NPC can act from information the Narrator prompt does not receive.
4. **Time and movement are flexible but fragile.** `world_time` and `arrival_world_time` are free text, so off-scene coordination depends on prompt discipline.
5. **Many recurring NPCs will strain the loop.** The current design is strong for a handful of active characters; larger casts need better scheduling, memory pruning, and relationship edges.

## Character Depth Philosophy

The goal is not just more NPC autonomy. The goal is for major NPCs to feel like real people rather than plot devices. They should seem to have existed before the player arrived and to continue existing after the player leaves the room.

The most important rule: every memorable character needs at least one meaningful contradiction. A loyal spouse may betray the player while still genuinely loving them. A zealous inquisitor may secretly doubt the creed. A brutal operator may be tender in one private relationship. Contradiction is more valuable than a longer list of traits because it creates pressure, hypocrisy, self-sabotage, and surprise.

Major NPC profiles should eventually carry this motivational core:

| Field | Purpose |
|---|---|
| `core_contradiction` | The unresolved tension that makes the NPC more than an archetype. |
| `primary_want` | The conscious desire they pursue. |
| `primary_fear` | The deeper terror that distorts their choices. |
| `secret` | The truth they hide from others, possibly including the player. |
| `misbelief` | The false belief that drives bad decisions. |
| `voice_pattern` | Speech rhythm, favorite phrases, register, and verbal habits. |
| `mannerisms` | Physical behavior under stress, anger, affection, boredom, or fear. |
| `emotional_tells` | What they do when lying, afraid, jealous, attracted, ashamed, or cornered. |

Example profile shape:

```text
Elara Voss
core_contradiction: She wants the security of the marriage while secretly undermining it.
primary_want: Security and status.
primary_fear: Abandonment and loss of control.
secret: She has been feeding information to a rival faction.
misbelief: The player does not truly love her and will leave once she is no longer useful.
voice_pattern: Warm, slightly formal, uses endearments, rarely says direct declarations of love.
mannerisms: Fidgets with her wedding ring when nervous.
emotional_tells: Goes still and over-polite when lying.
```

For v0.6.9, this remains design direction rather than migration scope. Some of the material can already live in `description`, `private_beliefs`, `relationship_to_player`, `long_term_agenda`, and `reveries`. The later v2 character-profile work should decide whether these become first-class `characters` columns, a separate `character_profiles` table, or row-based memories/beliefs with typed categories.

## Story Pressure Philosophy

The Narrator should be a strong story driver, not only a reactive prose renderer. The world should have inertia: threats move, rivals make decisions, secrets leak, deadlines tighten, and opportunities disappear even when the player hesitates.

The important distinction: the Narrator should create pressure, not railroad outcomes. Good pressure gives the player sharper choices. It introduces complications, consequences, revelations, deadlines, and emotional trade-offs while still letting the player's actions determine what happens next.

The story dossier should eventually carry active pressure, not just remembered facts:

| Field | Purpose |
|---|---|
| `urgency_level` | 1-10 pressure rating for prioritizing active threads. |
| `time_pressure` | A human-readable or structured deadline such as "deadline in 7 turns." |
| `escalation_risk` | What bad thing plausibly happens if this is ignored. |
| `dramatic_question` | The live question that makes the thread playable. |
| `narrator_prompt_hook` | A compact prompt-facing nudge such as "The cult is getting desperate and may attempt a bold move soon." |

The eventual Narrator stance should be:

- Never let the scene feel static; add new information, tension, movement, or consequence when the fiction supports it.
- Every few turns, escalate an existing story thread or introduce a side complication that grows from the dossier, NPC agendas, place, or prior choices.
- Use character contradictions and secrets to create natural conflict.
- Let the world continue moving when the player is passive, idle, or indecisive.
- Create interesting problems rather than only answering player input.
- Favor tension, mystery, dramatic irony, and meaningful trade-offs over comfortable slice-of-life drift.

The "complication engine" does not need to be a separate agent at first. It can begin as prompt structure and dossier fields: review active threads by urgency, review present NPC wants/fears/contradictions, then choose whether to add betrayal, revelation, external threat, rising personal stakes, unexpected alliance/rivalry, environmental event, or moral dilemma.

For v0.6.9, this is mostly prompt stance and future schema direction. The intent ledger supports it indirectly: once NPC plans and narrator outcomes are auditable, we can see whether story pressure is emerging from real NPC motives or just being pasted on top.

## Why

1. **Intent vs reality drift is invisible.** The NPC agent proposes actions, but only prompt text and turn metadata remember them; there is no queryable audit trail.
2. **NPC agency needs feedback.** If the narrator repeatedly ignores an NPC's plan, the next NPC agent tick should be able to react to that friction rather than pretending the plan happened.
3. **Future v2 work needs measurement.** Relationships, private memory, and narrator surprise are easier to evaluate once every planned action has an outcome label.
4. **Secrets need a safer foundation.** Before adding narrator-blind memory, we need a clear distinction between what an NPC intended, what the Narrator saw, and what became canon.
5. **Story pressure needs evidence.** Before adding clocks and complication jobs, we need to know whether agent-tier NPC motives are actually making it into narrator prose.

## Scope

One new table, a small addition to NPC agent output/application, narrator state formatting with intent IDs, and a post-narrator reconciliation step. No new UI is required for v0.6.9, though inspector surfacing is an obvious later patch.

The generic proposed schema (`characters`, `character_memories`, `character_goals`, `relationships`, `character_agency`, `main_story`, `events`) should not be copied wholesale. The current SQLite schema already covers most of that surface in project-local form:

| Generic concept | Current home | v0.6.9 action |
|---|---|---|
| Character identity/current state | `characters` | Keep as-is. Do not rebuild. |
| Character goals | `characters.active_goal`, `personal_goals`, `long_term_agenda`, `current_focus` | Keep as newline fields for now; row-based goals later if needed. |
| Memories/reveries | `memorable_facts`, `observations`, `recent_activity`, `private_beliefs`, `reveries` | Defer row-based memory with importance/decay/visibility. |
| Relationships | `relationship_to_player`, story threads of kind `relationship` | Defer first-class relationship edges. |
| Character contradiction/texture | `description`, `private_beliefs`, `long_term_agenda`, `reveries` | Defer first-class profile fields like `core_contradiction`, `misbelief`, `voice_pattern`, `mannerisms`, and `emotional_tells`. |
| Story pressure | `story_threads`, `story_objectives`, `story_clues`, `timeline_events` | Defer pressure fields like `urgency_level`, `time_pressure`, `escalation_risk`, `dramatic_question`, and `narrator_prompt_hook`. |
| Agency tier | `characters.agency_level` | Keep proximity vocabulary; use it when writing `npc_intents.agency_level`. |
| Main story | story dossier tables | Keep. |
| Events | `turns`, `timeline_events`, metadata | Add `npc_intents` for proposed-vs-canon actions. |

Schema migration:

```sql
CREATE TABLE npc_intents (
  id                    INTEGER PRIMARY KEY AUTOINCREMENT,
  world_id              INTEGER NOT NULL REFERENCES worlds(id) ON DELETE CASCADE,
  character_id          INTEGER NOT NULL REFERENCES characters(id) ON DELETE CASCADE,
  player_turn_id        INTEGER NOT NULL REFERENCES turns(id) ON DELETE CASCADE,
  narrator_turn_id      INTEGER REFERENCES turns(id) ON DELETE SET NULL,
  agency_level          TEXT NOT NULL,
  intent_text           TEXT NOT NULL,
  planned_action        TEXT NOT NULL,
  intent_type           TEXT,
  target_character_id   INTEGER REFERENCES characters(id) ON DELETE SET NULL,
  target_place_id       INTEGER REFERENCES places(id) ON DELETE SET NULL,
  private_rationale     TEXT,
  expected_visibility   TEXT NOT NULL DEFAULT 'narrator'
                        CHECK (expected_visibility IN ('public','narrator','npc_private','narrator_blind')),
  narrator_disposition  TEXT
                        CHECK (narrator_disposition IN ('staged','modified','ignored','contradicted')),
  narrator_interpretation TEXT,
  outcome_summary       TEXT,
  resolved_outcome      TEXT,
  reconciliation_confidence REAL
                        CHECK (reconciliation_confidence IS NULL OR
                               (reconciliation_confidence >= 0 AND reconciliation_confidence <= 1)),
  archived_patch        TEXT,
  created_at            TEXT NOT NULL DEFAULT (datetime('now')),
  updated_at            TEXT NOT NULL DEFAULT (datetime('now'))
);

CREATE INDEX npc_intents_world_turn ON npc_intents(world_id, player_turn_id);
CREATE INDEX npc_intents_character ON npc_intents(character_id, id);
CREATE INDEX npc_intents_pending ON npc_intents(world_id, narrator_turn_id)
  WHERE narrator_turn_id IS NULL;
```

## Decisions

| Decision | Choice | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| v0.6.9 slice | Ship `npc_intents` first | It directly addresses the clearest weakness and gives us evidence for later v2 changes. |
| Reconciliation owner | Start with a focused lightweight helper; reuse archivist context if cheap | The existing archivist already sees narrator prose and state, but intent classification is simpler than full world extraction. Keep the path swappable. |
| Narrator authority | Narrator may stage, modify, ignore, or contradict planned actions | Plans are character intent, not a hard script. The book remains canonical prose. |
| Private rationale | Store compact rationale, not broad `internal_thought` | We need audit/state, not hidden chain-of-thought transcripts. |
| Visibility field | Include `expected_visibility` now | It is cheap schema groundwork for narrator-blind/private-memory work later, but v0.6.9 does not enforce full visibility semantics. |
| Agency vocabulary | Keep `npc` / `local` / `nearby` / `distant` / `dormant` | The proposed 0-3 tier model is conceptually useful, but the current proximity-aware vocabulary is better for this codebase. |
| Model/provider naming | Use existing model configuration and prompt files | The source proposal says "Grok," but the milestone should stay provider-agnostic and match current code. |
| Confidence | Store optional reconciliation confidence | Low-confidence classification gives us a future hook for inspector flags or regeneration without blocking v0.6.9. |
| UI | No inspector UI in v0.6.9 | Database/tests first. Inspector surfacing can come once the shape stabilizes. |
| Package version | Bump to `0.6.9` only on the release branch | Matches repo convention; this planning doc does not bump `package.json` while current code is still `0.6.8`. |

## Prompt Stance

Keep the existing prompt files and add narrow clauses. Do not replace them with the generic v2 templates.

NPC agent additions:

- It may see recent intent outcomes, especially ignored or contradicted plans.
- It should return compact `intent_text`, concrete `planned_action`, optional target metadata, and optional compact private rationale.
- It should not output broad `internal_thought`.
- Its motivational core should weight current focus, personal goals, long-term agenda, private beliefs, reveries, relationship anchor, and recent outcomes.
- It should treat major NPCs as flawed, self-contradictory people: pursue want, flinch from fear, and allow hypocrisy, irrationality, shame, affection, and self-sabotage.
- It should reveal private life through concrete choices and subtext, not explanatory monologues.

Narrator additions:

- Planned moves are strong character-intent guidance.
- The narrator stages them naturally when possible.
- If a plan is impossible, contradicted by state, or overtaken by the player's move, the narrator may modify or fail it in prose.
- The narrator must never mention intent IDs or mechanics.
- The narrator receives only visibility-allowed planned actions; future narrator-blind memory must be filtered before this prompt, not merely hidden by instruction.
- When portraying NPCs, show contradictions through micro-actions, tone shifts, omissions, lies, and emotional tells rather than naming traits directly.
- Prioritize emotional authenticity over plot convenience when those conflict.
- The narrator is allowed to be an engine of drama: escalate existing threads, introduce organic complications, and keep the world moving when the player hesitates.
- Plot momentum must create meaningful choices, not forced outcomes.
- Use NPC agendas, story dossier pressure, secrets, contradictions, and unresolved tensions as the source of complications.

Future narrator prompt structure:

```markdown
**Dynamic Plot Generation**
The story must feel alive and cinematic. You have permission to advance multiple plot threads simultaneously.

Guidelines:
- Maintain 2-4 active major threads at any time.
- Introduce new hooks every 4-8 turns when the fiction has room.
- Use character contradictions and secrets to create drama.
- Escalate consequences when the player ignores problems.
- Create meaningful choices with real trade-offs.
- The world continues moving when the player is idle or indecisive.

Current Story Pressure:
{{story_dossier_with_pressure}}
```

## Explicit Cuts

- Generic replacement of the current SQLite design - rejected. Keep the existing schema and add focused pressure relief.
- `relationships` table - deferred to v0.7.0 or a later v0.6.x if intent audit proves stable.
- `character_memories` with `importance`, `decay_score`, and `visibility` - deferred until after intent audit.
- First-class character-depth fields such as `core_contradiction`, `primary_want`, `primary_fear`, `secret`, `misbelief`, `voice_pattern`, `mannerisms`, and `emotional_tells` - accepted direction, but deferred so v0.6.9 stays focused on intent/outcome audit.
- Story-pressure fields on dossier tables such as `urgency_level`, `time_pressure`, `escalation_risk`, `dramatic_question`, and `narrator_prompt_hook` - accepted direction, but deferred unless implementation discovers the intent audit needs one of them immediately.
- Separate complication engine agent/job - deferred. Start with prompt structure and later dossier fields before adding another moving part.
- Background off-screen story-thread simulation - deferred; likely belongs with background reverie or event-clock work.
- Enforced `narrator_blind` secrets - deferred until memory visibility has tests.
- Background reverie pass - deferred; keep it off the chat hot path.
- Structured `world_day` / `world_phase` clock - deferred; useful, but not required to audit intent drift.
- Inspector UI for intent rows - deferred to a follow-up patch.
- Max-10 high-agency NPC scoring policy - deferred unless v0.6.9 reveals the current tier scheduler over-selects.

## Accepted Tradeoffs

- **More write volume per turn.** Present agent NPCs create one extra row each. With a target of roughly 10 high-agency NPCs, this is tiny compared with turn prose and worth the auditability.
- **Reconciliation may be imperfect.** A lightweight classifier can mislabel subtle staging. The row remains useful because it stores the planned action, narrator turn, and outcome summary for debugging.
- **No immediate UI payoff.** The first value is internal observability and testability. Inspector display can follow once we trust the schema.
- **Visibility is schema-only at first.** `expected_visibility` prepares for narrator surprise but does not deliver full narrator-blind behavior in v0.6.9.
- **Intent rows may expose private motive in the database.** That is acceptable for developer audit, but prompt formatting must decide what each model sees. Do not assume storage visibility equals prompt visibility.

## Future v2 Direction

The broader v2 direction remains accepted, just not bundled into v0.6.9.

Planned next pieces:

- First-class `relationships` rows for trust, fear, leverage, promises, debts, secrets shared, and private sentiment.
- First-class major-NPC profile fields for contradiction, want, fear, secret, misbelief, voice, mannerisms, and emotional tells.
- Story-pressure fields on `story_threads`, `story_objectives`, and `story_clues`: urgency, deadline, escalation risk, dramatic question, and narrator prompt hook.
- Event clocks for major developments with semi-fixed timers, such as "the rival fleet arrives in 12 turns unless stopped."
- Consequence memory for unresolved tensions that should be fed back to the Narrator forcefully.
- Row-based `character_memories` with `importance`, `decay_score`, and `visibility`.
- Visibility filtering where the NPC agent can act from private/blind state while the narrator only receives visible behavior.
- Memory maintenance that keeps top important memories per character in active context and condenses old rows into beliefs, relationship history, or dossier entries.
- A stronger time model, likely additive: keep human-readable `world_time`, then add structured fields such as `world_day`, `world_phase`, or sortable elapsed minutes when the game needs deterministic ETA handling.
- Background reverie maintenance for dormant/distant NPCs, run manually or via Railway cron rather than every chat turn.

Important pushback preserved from the v2 review:

- Do not replace the current project prompts with generic "Westworld-style" sketches.
- Do not store broad model `internal_thought`.
- Do not use `narrator_blind` as a magic bucket for arbitrary plot truth.
- Do not let Agent state writes become canon until narrator prose and archivist reconciliation agree.

## Exit Criteria

1. `npm run lint` and `npm run type-check` pass.
2. `npm test` passes, including new intent persistence/reconciliation tests.
3. `package.json` version reads `0.6.9` on the release branch.
4. Fresh DB migration reaches v20 and `PRAGMA foreign_key_check` returns no rows.
5. Manual: create or use a world with a present local NPC, send an in-character turn, and verify `npc_intents` has one row per planned action with `player_turn_id`, `character_id`, and `planned_action`.
6. Manual: after the narrator response, verify the same rows have `narrator_turn_id` and a `narrator_disposition`.
7. Manual: force or observe a plan that the narrator changes; verify the row records `modified` or `ignored` rather than silently treating it as staged.
8. Manual: next NPC agent prompt includes recent outcome context for that NPC without exposing mechanics to the narrator prose.
9. Deploy to Railway and smoke one turn in prod without 5xx or migration errors.
10. Tag `v0.6.9` on `main`.

When all hold, v0.6.9 ships. Next likely milestone: relationship edges or memory visibility, depending on what the intent audit reveals.
