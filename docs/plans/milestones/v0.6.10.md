# v0.6.10 Milestone

**Target**: TBD
**Bar**: "When the player travels across places, the scene cursor follows them on the turn an NPC cluster arrives at the destination, and a stale scene anchor cannot survive more than one turn once travel is detectable in recent prose." (Deliberately *not* "the narrator can never rewind the protagonist": a bad archivist patch can still flip the player row backward — see the turn-403 counter-case — and the `world-state.ts` guard only inspects the last two narrator turns. The goal is fast detection and self-heal, not an absolute guarantee against a non-Claude narrator.)
**Budget**: ~3-6 hours focused work.

v0.6.9 shipped the NPC intent reconciler and the slimmer narrator prompt. v0.6.10 is a small patch addressing one concrete misfire surfaced in local play of "The Call-In Case" world on 2026-05-28: the player traversed home → Sacred Heart hospital across the seven narrator turns 389–401, but the archivist never advanced the protagonist's location (then snapped him back home at turn 403). `worlds.current_scene_id` stayed pinned at scene 1 (a bedroom) and the player character row stayed at place 30 (home). On a fresh narrator call with low prompt-cache reuse, the authoritative state block said "you're in the bedroom" while the recent-turn window said "you're confronting Cole in the IR anteroom"; the narrator trusted the pinned anchor and snapped the player back home, replaying the original phone call. No new tables, no new agents — this is a behaviour and prompting patch with one tiny invariant added in code.

**Corrected root cause (2026-05-28, after replaying the archivist patches in `turns.metadata`).** The original framing below ("scenes silently fail to advance") was only half the story and led to a fix keyed off the wrong field. Replaying the stored archivist patches for world 6, turns 389–403, shows the actual failure: **the archivist faithfully moved every NPC into the hospital across all seven travel turns (389–401) while never once setting the protagonist's `current_place_name`.** On turns 389/393/397/399/401 the patch included an `Andrew Osborne` row with `current_place_name` omitted (`None`); on 391/395 no player row at all; meanwhile Micha, Karen, Lisa Nguyen, Derek Holt, Priya Sharma, and R. Cole were all patched to `Sacred Heart Hospital — …`. At backup time the player row (id 40) is still `current_place_id = 30` (home) while every hospital NPC sits at place 31. The one turn that *did* set the player's place was turn 403 — and it set it **back to home**, ratifying the hallucination. So the stuck anchor was the player row's location, not merely the scene cursor; the scene never advanced precisely *because* the player's place never advanced. The strongest deterministic signal available — NPC-vs-player place divergence — was sitting in the patch data the whole time and is what the invariant must key off, not `characters[player].current_place_name` (the one field that was empty on every travel turn).

## Why

Three motivating observations from the "Call-In Case" investigation:

1. **The archivist moves NPCs but leaves the protagonist pinned.** Across turns 384–409 the prose went bedroom → car → east staff lot → locker room → IR room 7 → workstation → anteroom → patient. On every travel turn the archivist patched the *NPCs'* `current_place_name` to the hospital (creating place 31 and populating it with Micha, Karen, Lisa Nguyen, Derek Holt, Priya Sharma, R. Cole) but emitted the player's row with `current_place_name` omitted — or omitted the player row entirely. Because the player's place never moved, the scene-open infrastructure (`insertSceneStmt`, `setCurrentSceneStmt`, `setPlayersPlaceStmt` in `src/lib/archivist.ts:925-930`, fired from the `open` branch at `:1803`) was never invoked: no patch carried a `scene` field *and* none carried a player place change. The archivist prompt already instructs both (`prompts/archivist-system.md:25` "set the player character's `current_place_name` and open the scene there"; `:48` open on travel) — the agent (`grok-4.3`) simply disregarded it for the protagonist on all seven travel turns while applying it correctly to NPCs. So the failure is the agent dropping the *player's* location update, not a schema gap and not purely a scene-classification miss.
2. **A stale anchor in authoritative state overrides recent prose.** The narrator's STATE block renders the `Place:` line from the *active scene's* `place_id` (`src/lib/world-state.ts:111` — `currentPlace` is scene-derived, not read off the player row). When the player has moved but the scene cursor hasn't, the assembler sends a contradictory pair: stale pinned anchor vs. fresh recent turns. On the 2026-05-28 misfire the narrator (`grok-4.3`, only 128 cached input tokens of 5671) trusted the pinned anchor and produced turn 403, which re-opened in the original bedroom. The archivist then patched the player's `current_place_id` back to home and appended a contradictory `memorable_facts` line, ratifying the hallucination.
3. **There is no deterministic fallback when the patch contradicts itself.** Turns 389–401 each placed a cluster of NPCs at the hospital while the player row and scene stayed at home — a glaring divergence the system never acts on. Then turn 403's patch put Andrew back home with no scene action. The system has no invariant tying NPC-vs-player place divergence (or a player place flip) to a scene transition, so both the silent forward stall and the backward snap pass unchallenged. The available deterministic anchor is the divergence itself: when the patch parks present NPCs at a place the protagonist and scene are not, the protagonist has almost certainly moved there.

## Decisions

| Decision | Choice | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Where to enforce the invariant | Archivist application code, not the agent prompt alone | Prompts can be ignored under load or with non-Claude narrators. A deterministic invariant in TS is the floor; the prompt change is the ceiling. Belt-and-suspenders. |
| What the invariant keys off | NPCs *relocated this turn* diverging from the player/scene anchor, NOT `characters[player].current_place_name` | The replay proves the player place field was omitted on all 13 travel turns, so an invariant gated on it never fires. The NPC cluster *did* move — that divergence is the reliable signal that the protagonist travelled. Counting only NPCs the patch relocates (excluding no-op restatements like Jordana-still-at-home) makes it fire on the first travel turn (389) rather than lagging to 391. Direction-guarded so the backward turn-403 snap (player→home while NPCs at hospital) does not trigger it. |
| Which fix is primary | The invariant; `world-state.ts` is a narrow fallback | Earlier framing called the `world-state.ts` omission "what stops the snap-back" — but with the invariant firing at turn 389, the cursor is already correct by 403, so the `Place:` line is correct and the omission never even triggers in the fixed timeline. The invariant is primary for any destination with an NPC cluster. The `world-state.ts` omission earns its keep only for destinations the invariant cannot catch — solo/unpopulated travel (see tradeoffs) — and even there its 2-turn window is thin. State the division of labour honestly; do not bill `world-state.ts` as the main fix. |
| Auto-open title when archivist forgot to provide one | `"Arriving at <place>"`, no summary | Synthesised titles are easy to spot in the inspector and edit later. A bad auto-title is much better than a wrong scene anchor leaking into the narrator. |
| What to do with the `world-state.ts` anchor when prose disagrees | Omit, don't override | Omission keeps the prompt honest: the narrator reads place from recent prose, which is what it does well already. Overriding with a guessed place would shift the failure mode rather than remove it. |
| Should we backfill stale worlds | No | One-off DB repair is fine for the "Call-In Case" world (already done locally on 2026-05-28 — scenes 6/7/8 split, turns retagged). Production worlds will self-heal as new turns trigger the invariant. A backfill script is more risk than it's worth. |
| Do we need a new schema column to track "scene_drift_detected" events | No | The `console.warn` log is enough for v0.6.10. If drift is common after the fix, v0.6.11 can add a `world_corrections`-style audit row. |
| Should the archivist emit both close + open in one patch | Not in this version | The current archivist schema is single-action per patch (`prompts/archivist-system.md:48`). The auto-open path uses `autoCloseSceneStmt` for the prior scene, same as the existing manual `open` branch at `src/lib/archivist.ts:1810`. No schema change needed. |
| Where the invariant's writes run | Inside the existing `applyArchivistPatch` transaction | The invariant adds four writes (player place, auto-close, insert scene, set cursor). They must share the patch's transaction so a constraint failure mid-invariant cannot partial-commit — per the project rule to wrap multi-statement repairs in a single `BEGIN IMMEDIATE`. Confirm `applyArchivistPatch` is already transaction-wrapped before adding the block; if it is not, wrap it as part of this change. |

## Architecture sketch

```
narrator turn N (prose: player walks into IR room 7 with the team)
            │
            ▼
archivist patch  (what actually happened on turns 389-401)
  characters[NPCs...].current_place_name = "Sacred Heart Hospital - IR 7"
  characters[player].current_place_name = omitted    ◀── the real bug
  scene = omitted                                     ◀── consequence, not cause
            │
            ▼
applyArchivistPatch
  1. apply character updates → NPC place_ids change, player UNCHANGED (home)
  2. NEW INVARIANT: NPCs RELOCATED this turn cluster at place X (hospital);
       scene.place_id and player.current_place_id are both Y (home), X≠Y;
       scene action omitted; player is NOT being moved away from X
       → setPlayersPlaceStmt(X, world)            (move protagonist to NPC cluster)
       → autoCloseSceneStmt(prior scene, closed_at_turn = N)
       → insertSceneStmt(world, X, "Arriving at <place>", opened_at_turn = N)
       → setCurrentSceneStmt(new scene, world)
       → console.warn({world_id, turn_id, prior_place: Y, inferred_place: X, npcs})
            │
            ▼
next narrator turn sees scene anchor matching recent prose — no snap-back

(Counter-case — turn 403: player patched home while NPCs at hospital.
 NPC cluster X = hospital, but the patch moves the player AWAY from X, so the
 direction guard suppresses the invariant. The world-state.ts omission is what
 keeps the narrator from snapping back here.)
```

## Explicit cuts

- **Scene title quality.** Synthesised "Arriving at X" titles are functional, not literary. Better narrative-aware titles via a tiny LLM call are deferred to a later v0.6.x.
- **Place hierarchy / sub-locations.** "Sacred Heart Hospital — IR 7" vs "Sacred Heart Hospital — locker room" being treated as separate places is fine for now; a hierarchical place model is out of scope.
- **Drift audit table.** Logged-only for now. If logs show frequent drift, v0.6.11 adds a `scene_drift_events` row per occurrence.
- **Retroactive cleanup of other prod worlds.** Not in scope. New play heals itself; old worlds keep their pinned cursors unless the user repairs manually.
- **Conductor agent introducing scene cuts.** Still out of scope; scene transitions remain a downstream archivist responsibility, not an upstream director call.

## Accepted tradeoffs

- **The relocated-NPC heuristic has a real false-positive surface, and the obvious fixes break the Call-In case.** The invariant fires when the relocated NPCs cluster at a place X that differs from the scene and the player. The inverse of the Call-In scenario misfires: player and a nurse are in IR 7; the nurse steps out to the hallway. That is one relocated NPC at X = hallway ≠ scene ≠ player → majority-of-one → the invariant drags the player and the cursor out to the hallway, following an NPC the player did not go with. Same risk if the archivist places a *mentioned-but-off-scene* NPC ("Mike drove to the office") while the player is elsewhere. The direction guard does not help here — the player row is omitted, so there is nothing to guard. The trap is that the natural refinements each break turn 389: "require a majority of *prior-present* NPCs to move together" fails because the player was home *alone* and the hospital NPCs were never present with him; "fire only if no NPC remains at the old place" fails because Jordana stayed home. So place data alone cannot cleanly separate "the scene relocated and the player came along" from "one NPC left and the player stayed." **Decision for v0.6.10:** keep the heuristic as a *logged best-guess*, not a clean floor — rely on the `console.warn` + inspector edit for recovery, and keep the auto-opened scene cheaply reversible (synthesised title, no summary, prior scene auto-closed not deleted). If logs show real false positives, v0.6.11 adds a stronger signal (e.g. only fire when the relocated NPCs include one the player was just interacting with, or gate on player-action movement verbs).
- **A `close`-without-`open` turn will not trigger the invariant.** The trigger requires `patch.scene` omitted or `keep_open`. If the archivist closes the current scene on leaving a place but never opens the destination, NPC relocation that same turn is ignored and the world is left with no active scene until the next open. Rare (close-only is uncommon), but real; not handled in this version.
- **Auto-opened scenes will sometimes split a single beat in two.** The relocated-NPC vote fires on the first turn an NPC moves to the new place (verified: turn 389 on the Call-In trace, the same turn the prose enters the hospital). But if the narrator depicts travel across multiple turns with NO NPC relocating into the destination until partway through (e.g. the protagonist drives alone, NPCs only appear once inside), the invariant fires on the turn the first NPC lands, and the solo "drive over" beat lands inside the old scene. Tolerable — narratively this is invisible to the player. Note the corollary: **a turn where the protagonist travels somewhere genuinely unpopulated (an empty building, a deserted road) produces no relocated-NPC cluster, so the invariant never fires there** — that case relies entirely on the prompt fix and the `world-state.ts` omission — the deterministic floor has a hole for unpopulated destinations.
- **Synthesised titles look mechanical in the inspector.** The user will see "Arriving at Sacred Heart Hospital - IR 7" sitting next to lovingly-authored prior titles. The user can edit them in the inspector. The alternative — letting the cursor stay wrong — is worse.
- **`world-state.ts` omission is conservative.** Sometimes the prompt will drop a place line that was actually correct because the keyword check is fuzzy. The narrator reading place from recent prose is a strong fallback, so this rarely produces a visible defect, but it does reduce the explicit-state guardrail in those cases.

## Exit criteria

1. `npm run lint` and `npm run type-check` pass.
2. `npm test` passes, including the new `tests/archivist.test.ts` cases: the forward NPC-divergence auto-open and the backward direction-guard (turn-403 shape must NOT fire).
3. `package.json` and `package-lock.json` both read `0.6.10` on the release branch — bump on the release branch itself, not as a post-merge commit. The Chat header reads `pkg.version` so any gap between merge and bump means the header lies in prod.
4. `npm run dev` reproduction: start a new world whose opening scene is at place A, take 3-4 player turns that move clearly to a different named place B, confirm in Drizzle Studio / `sqlite3` that `worlds.current_scene_id` now points at a new scene whose `place_id` matches B, and that turns at B are tagged with that scene_id.
5. Repro check on a clone of the "Call-In Case" world's pre-fix state (`backups/chronicles.sqlite.pre-call-in-fix-20260528-122419`). **First reconstruct the turn-388 starting state — this is mandatory or the test silently no-ops.** That backup is the *post-bug* snapshot: place 31 already exists and the hospital NPCs (Micha/Karen/Cole/etc.) are already at `current_place_id = 31`. If you replay the 389-401 patches against it as-is, the "relocated this turn" check sees the NPCs already at the hospital → zero relocations → the invariant never fires and the repro falsely "passes by doing nothing". Before replaying, reset: player and all NPCs to place 30 (home), delete place 31, scene 6 active at place 30, `worlds.current_scene_id = 6`. THEN re-run the stored archivist patches for turns 389-401 through the new `applyArchivistPatch` and confirm the invariant fires on turn 389 (the first relocation; player + cursor advance to the hospital), stays put 391-401, and does NOT fire on turn 403 (player→home while NPCs at hospital). (A standalone replay during planning, seeded with the turn-388 state, already reproduced exactly this — fires once on 389, never on 403; fold that harness into `tests/` rather than leaving it ad hoc.) Then replay turn 402's player input under v0.6.10 logic and verify the next narrator turn stays at the hospital (no bedroom snap-back). If the snap-back still occurs even with the scene cursor correct, the `world-state.ts` omission needs another pass.
6. Deploy → production smoke test — Andrew confirms a new prod world traversing two places ends with `current_scene_id` at the destination scene, not the origin.
7. Tag `v0.6.10` on `main`.

When all 7 hold, v0.6.10 ships.
