# v0.6.19 — Emergent NPCs (interjection + awakening), on a coherent foundation

**Status:** design approved (brainstorming), pre-implementation-plan.
**Predecessor:** v0.6.18 (living-NPCs substrate — `npc_reveries`, deterministic flares, daily loops, `worldTimeBand`).
**Origin:** the emergent-behavior layer foreshadowed in the v0.6.18 spec's "v0.6.19 seam", **plus** three production bugs found in the live world *The Violet Exchange* (world 13) that block it.

## Summary

Make NPCs feel alive by letting them **insert themselves into the player's story** — and the more the player thwarts an NPC's authored arc, the more it stirs, acts out, and reaches in. This is the headline.

Investigating *The Violet Exchange* before designing the emergent layer surfaced three bugs that are prerequisites, not side quests: a "living" NPC that teleports, acts omnisciently, or narrates a choice-menu would make the feature worse, not better. So v0.6.19 ships in two stages:

- **Stage A — Foundations.** Fix the three bugs as a coherent base: an anti-teleport scene-transition invariant, a lightweight NPC knowledge boundary, and 4th-wall prose hardening. Independently shippable and valuable on its own.
- **Stage B — Emergent layer.** On top of A: a dissonance→awakening track and turn-driven proactive interjection (present-scene and off-scene), with awakened NPCs escalated to the narrator-tier model.

If B slips, A still lands real prod fixes.

### Guiding principle

The system — not the LLM — decides what each character knows, where they are, and when they act. v0.6.18 made the living-NPC layer deterministic and reproducible wherever it could; v0.6.19 keeps that bias: deterministic invariants in code where correctness matters (geography, knowledge gating, dissonance accumulation), LLM agency only where texture matters (the interjection decision itself).

### Decisions (locked during brainstorming)

1. **Scope:** awakening **and** proactive contact, together — NPCs interjecting into the player's story is the point.
2. **Trigger:** the npc-agent *decides* whether to interject (agentic), rather than a deterministic gate. (Trade-off: leans on a model that under-emits structured directives; mitigated by B4 escalation + a deterministic soft cap.)
3. **Cadence:** agent's discretion, **soft-capped** — no fixed per-turn count, only a ceiling that prevents runaway and protects the 8K narrator budget.
4. **Channels:** **both** present-scene interjections **and** off-scene arrivals (walk in / phone / knock / message).
5. **Awakening:** dissonance **both** fuels interjection (thwarted → acts out, bolder) **and** independently colors ordinary narration.
6. **Model/cost:** escalate **only awakened** NPCs to the narrator-tier model; routine ticks stay on Haiku.
7. **Knowledge model:** **lightweight boundary** — no new perception ledger; stop broadcasting cross-NPC private knowledge, fix co-presence, add an archivist perception check.

### Out of scope (explicitly deferred)

- **Off-screen *world*-advancement director** — the scoped "director" from the seam (seam piece 3). Off-scene NPCs still only advance via the existing npc-agent tick + `daily_loop`/`last_known_situation`.
- **Full channel-aware perception ledger** — A2 is the lightweight version; a per-character, per-channel knowledge store is a later milestone.
- **Async / scheduled push** — interjection stays **turn-driven**: it surfaces on the player's next turn, reusing the momentum/narrator path. No scheduler, no out-of-band notification.
- **Story-breaking awakening** — the ceiling is subtle, bounded coloring (hesitate, question, misremember, act slightly against goal). An NPC never fully abandons the authored story without a tunable config knob.
- **Dropping `characters.reveries`** — still-dormant column; later cleanup.

# Stage A — Foundations

The three bugs were root-caused against world 13's play history (turns 993–1061) and the live code paths. Each had a guardrail that *already existed in a prompt* and was not honored by the Haiku archivist; A turns the load-bearing ones into deterministic code.

## A1 — Anti-teleport / scene-transition invariant (Bug 1)

### Problem

In world 13 the player improvised travel ("Abby pulls up in a van… head back to the safe house"). The archivist created place 68 **"En route to safe house"** — a *transit pseudo-place* the archivist prompt explicitly forbids (`prompts/archivist-system.md:11`) — and opened scene 19 there. When the player then entered the safe house (t:1010–1011), **no new scene/place was opened**, so scene 19 stayed pinned to the "en route" place through t:1045. With the anchor reading as in-transit, the narrator snapped the whole cast back into the moving van to stage the next beat (t:1029), and location oscillated car ↔ safe house ↔ alley thereafter.

This is the v0.6.10 snap-back class. The archivist prompt already predicts it almost verbatim (`prompts/archivist-system.md:53`: *"a player location change without a scene action leaves the cursor pinned to the old anchor, and the narrator will later snap the protagonist back to it"*). Haiku simply did not comply.

### Fix — deterministic invariant, not prompt-only

Post-archivist, in the patch-application path (`src/app/api/chat/route.ts`, where the archivist patch is reconciled into world state):

1. **Auto-open on location change.** If the applied patch changes the player character's `current_place_name` (vs the previous turn) but contains no `scene.action: "open"`, synthesize a scene-open at the new place. The cursor can never lag the prose by a turn again.
2. **Normalize/reject transit pseudo-places.** A place whose name matches a transit pattern (`/^en route to /i`, `/ - en route to /i`, `/^not (yet )?at /i`, `/^heading to /i`, `/^on the way to /i`) collapses to its destination (the trailing place name) if that resolves to a known place, otherwise the place update is dropped and the prior anchor held. This enforces `archivist-system.md:11` in code.
3. **Prompt reinforcement (secondary).** Tighten the archivist examples so an arrival within a player-authored "travel + arrive in one turn" beat is shown closing the transit and opening the destination scene — but the code invariant is the real guarantee.

### Interaction with the existing v0.6.10 suppression

`formatStateBlock` already suppresses a stale Place line when recent prose travelled elsewhere (`world-state.ts:260-263`). With A1 fixing the cursor at the source, suppression becomes a rarely-hit fallback again rather than the primary defense — which is its intended role per its own comment. No change needed there beyond confirming it still fires only as a fallback.

## A2 — Lightweight NPC knowledge boundary (Bug 2)

### Problem

`formatStateBlock` injects **every present NPC's `private_beliefs` and `observations`** into the single shared narrator prompt (`world-state.ts:317-325, 352-356`). The narrator therefore sees each NPC's private knowledge and can let any NPC act on any other's. "Present" is computed purely from a shared `current_place_id` (`world-state.ts:143`), so a radio voice counts as in-the-room. In world 13 the teleport collapsed the whole crew onto place 68, making them mutually omniscient: Marcus, with his radio off (t:1035), still "knew" an order given over the dead channel and "heard" a suppressed shot two blocks away. The Haiku archivist also authors observations omnisciently (it sees the whole turn), recording perceptions the character could not have had.

CLAUDE.md's stated rule — *"the system decides what it remembers"* — is not enforced at the per-character level.

### Fix — boundary without a ledger

1. **Stop broadcasting cross-NPC private knowledge.** In `formatStateBlock`, do not emit other NPCs' `private_beliefs` into the shared narrator prompt as freely as today. Surface a present NPC's belief sparingly and tagged as *that NPC's private view*, and add a hard narrator rule: **"Each NPC knows only what it has perceived. Never let one NPC act on another NPC's private belief, or on an event it did not witness."**
2. **Separate physical presence from channel reach.** Distinguish *Present* (same `place_id`) from *On comms* (reachable via an open radio/phone channel) in the state block. The narrator may have an off-scene NPC speak over a channel, but must not treat that voice as physically present or visually aware of the scene. Co-presence for the purpose of injecting beliefs/observations is gated on true physical presence, not channel.
3. **Archivist perception check.** Add a rule to `prompts/archivist-system.md`: record an NPC `observation` only if the NPC could actually sense the event (same place, or an open audio channel for things audible). No observations authored from the narrator's omniscient view of events the NPC could not perceive.

This is a **boundary**, not a simulation: it removes the leak vectors and instructs against omniscience, without building a per-character/per-channel knowledge store (deferred).

## A3 — 4th-wall / prose hardening (Bug 3)

### Problem

At t:1053 the narrator produced an explicit option menu: *"From here the options are narrow and immediate: the car where Titus still sits…, the radio in the safe house…, or the network of side streets… if you choose to track Marcus's likely path."* Plus a recurring objective-reminder coda ("the bridge waiting", "leaves a clear next step hanging"). The narrator prompt bans specific mechanics words ("quest", "objective", "hook", "clue") but not the *menu* pattern or *"if you choose to…"* construction, so Grok slipped the denylist. The per-turn injection of `activeObjective/Clue/Threat` titles (`route.ts:293-301`) feeds the coda tic.

### Fix

1. **Narrator prompt:** add an explicit prohibition on **option menus and choice framing** — "your options are…", "from here you could…", "you can either…", "if you choose to…", any enumerated list of possible actions — alongside the existing fourth-wall rule. The narrator presents a *situation*, never a menu.
2. **Objective-reminder coda:** reframe the `activeObjective/Clue/Threat` titles fed at `route.ts:293-301` as narrator-only pressure that must **never** be surfaced as a list or named as a goal; consider trimming what's passed each turn. The narrator may let pressure bend a scene, but must not recite the dossier back to the player.

# Stage B — Emergent layer

Rides on A. The closed loop: a thwarted NPC accumulates dissonance, which both colors its ordinary behavior and — once it "awakens" — escalates its model and licenses it to interject, present or off-scene; the narrator renders it; the reconciler labels how it landed, feeding dissonance again.

```
player turn
  → narrator renders (may contradict/ignore an NPC's plan or interjection)
  → intent-reconciler labels each NPC intent's disposition          [exists, npc-intents.ts]
        contradicted / ignored → + dissonance
        staged / modified (honored) → slight decay
  → dissonance threshold → awakening_state (dormant → stirring → awake)
  → next turn:
       • COLORING (always, deterministic): stirring/awake → STATE subtext line  (like reverie flares)
       • INTERJECTION (awake): npc-agent tick escalates to narrator-tier model,
         decides whether & how to insert itself (present / off-scene arrival),
         gated by A2's knowledge boundary and A1's stable geography
```

## B1 — Dissonance → awakening (the engine)

- **Signal.** The reconciler already records `narrator_disposition ∈ {staged, modified, ignored, contradicted}` per NPC intent (`src/lib/npc-intents.ts`, `src/lib/intent-reconciler.ts`). `contradicted`/`ignored` add dissonance; `staged`/`modified` decay it slightly. A lighter, deterministic **loop-deviation** add-on (NPC narrated acting against its `daily_loop` band) is optional additive signal, kept simple.
- **Accumulation.** Pure function: `nextDissonance(prev, dispositionsThisTurn, deviation) → number`, with conservative weights and per-turn decay. Unit-tested.
- **State.** Stored on `characters.dissonance` (REAL). `characters.awakening_state` derived by threshold (`dormant` / `stirring` / `awake`) and stored for the inspector. Zero LLM calls.

## B2 — Awakening coloring (always-on)

Deterministic STATE-block line for `stirring`/`awake` NPCs instructing the narrator to color them with subtext (hesitation / misremembering / slight defiance), modeled exactly on the v0.6.18 reverie-flare line in `formatStateBlock`. Bounded by the ceiling config knob; never story-breaking.

## B3 — Proactive interjection (headline)

- **Decision (agentic).** The npc-agent (`src/lib/npc-agent.ts`) may emit a new intent of `intent_type = "interjection"` with a `channel` (present: *speak / act*; off-scene: *arrive / phone / knock / message*), a target (reuse existing `target_character_id` / `target_place_id`), and a rationale. Driven by goals + `daily_loop` + dissonance.
- **Plausibility.** Off-scene arrival rides v0.6.18's `daily_loop` + `worldTimeBand`: an NPC can plausibly show up where its routine + location allow — but an **awake** NPC may *break* routine to reach the player (the break is the drama). The narrator arbitrates final plausibility (it may `ignore`/`contradict`, which raises dissonance — closing the loop).
- **Soft cap.** A deterministic ceiling on interjection directives surfaced per turn (budget guard for the 8K narrator input), with `characters.last_interjection_turn_id` providing a per-NPC rate limit. The agent has discretion under the cap; the cap is non-negotiable. **Any drop is logged** (no silent truncation).
- **Surfacing.** Selected interjection directives are injected into the narrator STATE block; the narrator writes the prose; the reconciler records the disposition next pass.
- **Knowledge.** A2 gates what an interjecting NPC may know/reference.

## B4 — Model escalation

Routine npc-agent ticks stay on Haiku (`NPC_AGENT_MODEL`, `claude-haiku-4-5`). When a character's `awakening_state` is `awake`, its tick escalates to the **narrator-tier model** — the same higher-capability model the narrator already runs on (`NARRATOR_MODEL`, currently Grok via `xai(...)` in `route.ts`), not Haiku — for a reliable agentic decision. The exact escalation target is confirmed during planning, but the intent is "the strong model, not the cheap one." Cost scales with drama, and the fiction (awake ⇒ more capable of acting out) matches the mechanism. Token usage tracked in `turns.metadata` as today.

## Data model — migration 25

(Migration 24 added `npc_reveries` + `characters.daily_loop`; next is 25.)

- `characters.dissonance` REAL DEFAULT 0
- `characters.awakening_state` TEXT (nullable; derived/stored, defaults to `dormant`)
- `characters.last_interjection_turn_id` INTEGER (nullable; per-NPC interjection rate limit)
- `npc_intents.channel` TEXT (nullable)
- `intent_type` gains a recognized value `interjection` (no schema change if `intent_type` is free TEXT — confirm during planning)

`npc_reveries.is_cornerstone` already exists (v0.6.18) and remains available for future cornerstone-memory work; v0.6.19 does not require it.

## Testing

- **Pure functions (deterministic, unit-tested):**
  - A1: transit-place normalization (`en route to X` → `X`); "location changed without scene-open → synthesize open" decision.
  - A2: present-vs-on-comms partition; "is this event perceivable by NPC N?" helper.
  - B1: `nextDissonance` accumulation/decay; threshold → `awakening_state` band.
  - B3: soft-cap selection (which interjections surface, what gets dropped + logged); off-scene arrival plausibility from `daily_loop` + `worldTimeBand`.
- **Migration 25 test:** columns present with defaults; existing rows backfilled to `dissonance=0`, `awakening_state='dormant'`.
- **Wiring test:** reconciler dispositions → dissonance update on the right character.
- **Regression (world-13 shapes):** a player-authored "travel + arrive in one turn" beat produces a destination scene, not a transit pseudo-place, and the next narrator turn does not snap back to the vehicle.
- **Browser end-to-end:** repeatedly thwart an NPC → `awakening_state` advances → it interjects (one present-scene, one off-scene arrival) within budget → inspector shows `dissonance` / `awakening_state` / recent interjection; a radio-only NPC does not demonstrate in-room awareness.

## Cost / budget

- A1, A2 structured-state changes, B1 dissonance, B2 coloring: deterministic, **zero new LLM calls**.
- B3 interjection: no new call — folded into the existing npc-agent tick. The only cost delta is B4: **awake** ticks run on the narrator-tier model instead of Haiku. Bounded by how many NPCs are awake and the soft cap.
- 8K narrator input budget preserved: interjection directives are bounded by the soft cap; A2 *reduces* state-block tokens (less cross-NPC belief spam).

## Risk — intent layer must actually run (gates Stage B)

`npc_intents` was **empty** for world 13 (`COUNT=0`), even though characters have `agency_level` set. Stage B's entire loop (dissonance from dispositions, interjection intents, reconciliation) assumes the npc-agent + reconciler are producing rows. **Before building B, an implementation-plan task must determine why world 13 recorded zero intents** — imported-world quirk (FK remap / agent never ticked post-import), an agency/config gate, or a silent insert failure — and confirm the loop fires on a normally-created world. If the layer is broken, fixing it is a Stage-B prerequisite.
